Drop Earrings

Not Bombs 

 

HandCrafted with Love

BY SYRIAN REFUGEES

 

Crafted in Istanbul, Turkey, Drop Earrings Not Bombs is a project that raises funds and generates income for the Syrian refugees of war living in Istanbul. 

 

Partnering with Small Projects Istanbul's Olive Tree Women's Craft Collective, Drop Earrings Not Bombs earrings are handmade by Syrian refugees residing in Istanbul.  Each pair of earrings is a unique display of creativity and design, a story told through the eyes of a person who has been through more than any one life should experience.  The project creates a space for color therapy, community building and artistic expression.  100% of the proceeds from the Drop Earrings Not Bombs project goes back into Small Projects Istanbul, where the refugees are provided with access to supplemental education and programs that assist students and families from Syria succeed in Turkey and beyond, paving the way for better futures.

THE OLIVE TREE WOMEN'S CRAFT COLLECTIVE

Small Projects Istanbul runs a craft collective at its Olive Tree community center in Fatih, Istanbul, where Syrian refugee women have the opportunity to develop skills in hand crafts and earn livelihood support to help them rebuild their lives.

ABOUT SMALL PROJECTS ISTANBUL

The future of Syria may be uncertain, but the future for students and families from Syria doesn’t have to be.
The mission of Small Projects Istanbul (SPI) is to provide access to formal as well as supplemental education that will assist students and families from Syria to succeed in Turkey and beyond, paving the way for better opportunities in the future.
As a global community of volunteers and supporters, SPI is working to provide supplemental education for children through weekly Turkish language classes and a homework support club, as well as art and other activities that develop community and a new sense of belonging at the Olive Tree Community Center. SPI is also running a scholarship fund to ensure primary and high school students can re-enter formal education, whether in Turkish or Syrian schools.
For adults, there are Turkish, German, English and TOEFL classes at the community center.
SPI believes helping to address the current crisis of education for children and young people from Syria will help entire families get back on their feet, and help create the new generation of leaders needed for Syria’s future.
 
 
 
Meet some of the participants in the Drop Earrings Not Bombs Project. These are some of the artisans who create the earrings and who are at the heart of the project.
Hiba
Hiba is the youngest participant in the Drop Earrings Not Bombs project. She left her home in Aleppo for Istanbul with her family in 2013. She was 13 years old at the time, the second of five siblings. For two years she attended Al-Nokhba, a makeshift school for Syrian refugees. However, now at 16 years of age, the school does not offer any classes at her grade level. Instead, she regularly attends courses at Small Projects Istanbul where she has a new community of friends and learning opportunities. She is interested in becoming an interior designer.
Sawsan
Sawsan came to Istanbul two years ago to join her son who made the same journey before her. Sawsan's husband, a plumber, stayed behind in Damascus for financial reasons. She is very eager to work. When the situation in Syria affected her family financially she started working as a caretaker of the elderly. She enjoys participating in Drop Earrings Not Bombs workshops and hopes that the project will take off so she can have more earrings to create.
Farida
Farida and her family came to Istanbul from Aleppo six months ago. She is the proud mother of three children, ages 3, 9, and 11. In Syria she worked as a housewife while her husband provided for them as an Arabic teacher. He struggles to find work now. She enjoys working on the earrings, stating that, "it forces her to challenge herself artistically."
Nina
Nina was born in Baghdad and lived in Damascus from when she was 4 years old. She relocated to Istanbul with her mother and two sisters this year, at age 17. Her father still resides in Baghdad and supports the family financially. Adjusting to Istanbul and the Turkish language has been difficult for Nina. She expresses herself through her love for heavy metal, always wearing black clothes and metal ornaments, and she meets everyone in the workshop with the kindest smile.
Zeyna, Lin, Qotaiba and Mahmoud
Zeyna, Lin, Qotaiba and Mahmoud are all from the same family, made Istanbul their home four years ago. Back in Syria Mahmoud was, among many things, a jeweler, designing his own creations with his wife Zeyna at night and selling them in his shop during the day. When the bombs started to hit the city they lived in, Mahmoud travelled alone to Istanbul where he found work as a tour guide. After securing a home for his family, Mahmoud's parents, wife Zeyna, and their two children came to join him. Eventually so did his 6 brothers and their wives, including Lin and Qotaiba. Most of the brothers were able to earn a decent living in the tourism industry until the terror incident that occurred in January. Now they are very active in the Drop Earrings Not Bombs project, especially Mahmoud who plays an integral role in communicating and coordinating the work with project participants, as well as handling shipping.

Orders

Drop Earrings Not Bombs is running on a donation basis.  For every donation, donors will receive earrings.  The money donated goes to Small Projects Istanbul and not only supports the women involved in the Olive Tree Women's Craft Collective, but it also supports the activities and refugee families who are a part of the Small Projects Istanbul community.

If you would like to order earrings or ornaments, you can donate via the PayPal button below or by wire transfer personal donation.
Please contact us at dropearringsnotbombs@gmail.com to notify us of the order and include your confirmation of donation, mailing address, and preference for gold or silver frames.  You are welcome to request specific models and/or color combinations.
